# US-045: Define Staff Role & Permissions

**Story ID:** US-045  
**Epic:** EP-005 (User Access & Role Management)  
**Priority:** HIGH  
**Story Points:** 3  
**Owner Role:** Security / Platform Engineer  
**Status:** Draft

## User Story

As a **clinic operations manager**, I want the Staff role to access patient appointments, check-in workflows, and queue management only for assigned providers so that staff can perform daily tasks while protecting patient confidentiality.

## Business Value

- Empowers staff to complete operational tasks securely
- Limits unnecessary exposure of patient data
- Reduces risk of internal PHI misuse

## Scope

In scope:
- Staff access to same-day queue, walk-in appointments, check-in, and related patient details
- Data scoping to assigned providers, clinics, or care teams
- Time-limited session enforcement for staff operations

Out of scope:
- Admin-level user/account management
- Access to full patient audit logs (admin-only)

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Given a Staff user is authenticated, when they view the appointment queue, then they only see appointments for their assigned provider or team.
2. Given a Staff user opens patient details, then they see only the information needed for check-in and visit operations.
3. Given a Staff user attempts to access another provider's queue or unrelated patient record, then the system returns 403 Forbidden.
4. Given a Staff user performs check-in, then the action is logged with staff ID, provider assignment, and timestamp.
5. Given a Staff user has no active assignment, then access to patient operations is denied.

## Technical Notes

- Enforce assignment-based data filters in staff-facing endpoints.
- Implement middleware to resolve current staff provider/team context.
- Audit all staff access and actions with role and assignment metadata.

## Dependencies

- RBAC model from US-043.
- Provider/provider-team assignment data from scheduling system.

## Definition of Done

- Staff role permissions implemented and validated.
- Assignment-based access filtering in place.
- Authorization failures properly logged and tested.
- Security review completed for staff access controls.
