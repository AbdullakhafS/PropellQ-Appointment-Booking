# US-080: Document Data Retention & Deletion Policies

**Story ID:** US-080  
**Epic:** EP-007 (Compliance & Audit Logging)  
**Priority:** HIGH  
**Story Points:** 3  
**Owner Role:** Compliance / Security Engineer  
**Status:** Draft

## User Story

As a **compliance officer**, I want documented data retention and deletion policies so that the organization can enforce consistent handling of PHI and audit logs.

## Business Value

- Clarifies retention requirements and lifecycle controls
- Reduces risk of improper data deletion or over-retention
- Supports HIPAA compliance and audit readiness

## Scope

In scope:
- Retention policy definitions for PHI and audit logs
- Deletion rules and approval controls
- Storage lifecycle and archival guidance

Out of scope:
- Technical implementation of all archival tiers (covered in other stories)

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Given PHI and audit data, then retention periods are documented clearly and aligned to compliance requirements.
2. Given deletion workflows, then the criteria for deletion and approval are documented.
3. Given retention and deletion policies, then the organization can reference them during audits.
4. Given a policy update, then change history is documented and version-controlled.

## Technical Notes

- Define minimum and maximum retention periods where applicable.
- Include procedures for review and approval of deletion operations.
- Align with legal and regulatory guidance.

## Dependencies

- Audit log retention and storage design from EP-007.
- Compliance team guidance.

## Definition of Done

- Retention and deletion policies documented and versioned.
- Policies reviewed by compliance stakeholders.
- Policies referenced in audit readiness materials.
