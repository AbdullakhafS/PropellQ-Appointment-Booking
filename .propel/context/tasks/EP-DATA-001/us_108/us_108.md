# US-108: Build Backup/Restore Automation and Verification

**Story ID:** US-108  
**Epic:** EP-DATA-001 (Data Platform & Governance)  
**Priority:** CRITICAL  
**Story Points:** 8  
**Owner Role:** Database Engineer / SRE  
**Status:** Draft

## User Story

As an operations engineer, I want automated backup/restore workflows with verification so that data can be recovered within target objectives during incidents.

## Business Value

- Protects business continuity for critical patient and appointment data.
- Reduces downtime and recovery uncertainty during failures.
- Provides auditable evidence that recovery objectives are achievable.

## Scope

In scope:
- Automated full and incremental backup schedules.
- Encrypted backup storage and access controls.
- Periodic restore drills in isolated environment.
- Backup integrity checks and restore-time validation.
- Recovery runbooks and RPO/RTO evidence capture.

Out of scope:
- Cross-region active-active failover design.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Given production data stores, when backup jobs run, then full and incremental backups complete per schedule with success metrics recorded.
2. Given backup artifacts, when stored, then encryption at rest and strict access controls are enforced.
3. Given monthly recovery drill, when restore is executed in isolated environment, then restore completes within defined RTO and data currency meets RPO target.
4. Given restored datasets, when validation scripts run, then integrity checks (counts, checksums, referential sanity) pass.
5. Given backup or restore failures, when detected, then alerting and incident runbook workflows trigger.
6. Given audit requests, when presented, then backup logs, restore drill evidence, and control approvals are retrievable.

## Technical Notes

- Target requirements: DR-003, DR-004, NFR-004, NFR-006.
- Include point-in-time recovery capability where supported.
- Ensure backup keys and credentials follow secret management standards.

## Dependencies

- Data lifecycle baseline from US-106.
- Environment/secret standards from EP-TECH-001 (US-103).

## Risks and Mitigations

- Risk: Backups complete but are not restorable.
- Mitigation: Mandatory recurring restore drills and automated integrity verification.

## Definition of Done

- Backup automation enabled with monitoring and alerts.
- Restore drill cadence established and executed successfully.
- RPO/RTO measurements documented and approved.
- Incident recovery runbook updated and tested.

## INVEST Check

- Negotiable: Yes, tooling and schedule details can vary.
- Valuable: Yes, core resilience and compliance value.
- Estimable: Yes, concrete automation and drill deliverables.
- Small: Medium-large but manageable in phased sprint work.
- Testable: Yes, through scheduled restore verification.
