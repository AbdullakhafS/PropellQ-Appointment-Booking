# US-106: Implement Retention and Archive Lifecycle Jobs

**Story ID:** US-106  
**Epic:** EP-DATA-001 (Data Platform & Governance)  
**Priority:** CRITICAL  
**Story Points:** 8  
**Owner Role:** Data Engineer / Compliance Engineer  
**Status:** Draft

## User Story

As a compliance engineer, I want automated retention and archive jobs so that regulated data is retained, archived, and purged according to policy without manual intervention.

## Business Value

- Ensures compliance with retention obligations and legal controls.
- Reduces storage growth and operational costs via lifecycle automation.
- Improves audit readiness with deterministic, repeatable retention behavior.

## Scope

In scope:
- Policy-driven retention schedules by data domain.
- Archive jobs for aged operational records.
- Immutable retention handling for audit logs.
- Purge jobs for eligible data with legal-hold exceptions.
- Job monitoring and failure alerting.

Out of scope:
- Historical reclassification of all legacy records.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Given retention policies are configured, when lifecycle jobs run, then records move to archive or purge state according to policy windows.
2. Given audit log datasets, when retention is applied, then immutable retention windows are enforced and deletion is blocked before expiry.
3. Given records under legal hold, when purge jobs execute, then held records are excluded and logged.
4. Given lifecycle jobs fail, when incidents occur, then alerts trigger and retries follow configured backoff.
5. Given archived records, when retrieval is requested by authorized role, then retrieval path is documented and verifiable.
6. Given compliance review, when evidence is requested, then lifecycle execution reports and policy versions are available.

## Technical Notes

- Target requirements: DR-003, NFR-006, NFR-004.
- Include time-zone and date-boundary handling for policy windows.
- Keep lifecycle state transitions idempotent.

## Dependencies

- Data model and partition strategy from US-104.
- Compliance controls from EP-007.

## Risks and Mitigations

- Risk: Accidental purge of active records.
- Mitigation: Dry-run mode, threshold guardrails, and approval gate for destructive actions.

## Definition of Done

- Retention/archive/purge jobs deployed with monitoring.
- Legal hold rule integration verified.
- Compliance report export available per execution cycle.
- Recovery procedure documented for lifecycle job failures.
