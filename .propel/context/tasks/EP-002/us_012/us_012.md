# US-012: Design Multi-Step Chatbot Conversation Flow

**Epic:** EP-002 (Patient Intake & Insurance Verification)  
**Story Points:** 5  
**Priority:** HIGH  
**Status:** Not Started  
**Created:** 2026-06-17  

---

## User Story Statement

As a **product designer**, I want to **define a conversational flow that guides patients through intake in a logical, empathetic sequence without overwhelming them with questions**, so that **the chatbot experience feels natural, completion rate stays ≥85%, and patient satisfaction is ≥4.5/5.0**.

---

## Business Value

- **Outcome 1:** Well-designed conversation flow increases completion rate from 40% (forms) to 85%+ (AI)
- **Outcome 2:** Empathetic sequencing reduces patient anxiety; improves NPS
- **Outcome 3:** Logical Q&A order captures higher-quality data (context-aware responses)

---

## Scope

**In Scope:**
- Define conversation stages: greeting, chief complaint, history, medications, allergies, insurance, summary, confirmation
- Branching logic: If-then rules (e.g., if patient says "chest pain," ask follow-up about duration/severity)
- Conditional questions: Skip irrelevant questions based on previous answers
- Tone/personality: Define chatbot voice (professional but warm, not corporate)
- Conversation length target: 5-7 minutes average (user research validated)
- Question phrasing: A/B test different wordings for clarity
- Visual cues: Conversation progress indicator (e.g., "Step 2 of 6")
- Fallback paths: If patient can't answer, offer to skip or switch to manual

**Out of Scope:**
- Implementation of branching logic (handled in US-011)
- NLP model training (handled in US-011)
- Chatbot personality beyond conversational tone (full personality design = Phase 2)

---

## Acceptance Criteria

1. **Conversation flow document created:** Detailed flowchart or Figma spec defining all conversation stages, questions, branches, and transitions
2. **Opening greeting:** Chatbot greets patient by name, explains intake purpose, gives time estimate: "Hi [Name], I'll help you complete your intake in about 5-7 minutes. Ready to start?"
3. **Chief complaint stage (1-2 min):** 
   - Q1: "What brings you in today?" (open-ended)
   - Q2 (conditional): If patient mentions symptom, ask follow-ups: "How long have you had [symptom]?" or "How severe is it on a scale of 1-10?"
4. **Medical history stage (1-2 min):**
   - Q3: "Do you have any chronic conditions? (diabetes, asthma, heart disease, high blood pressure)" (checkbox list fallback)
   - Q4 (conditional): If patient mentioned condition, ask: "How long have you had [condition]?" and "What medications do you take for it?"
5. **Current medications stage (1 min):**
   - Q5: "What medications are you currently taking?" (open-ended; patient lists)
   - Q6 (conditional): For each medication mentioned, ask: "What's the dosage and how often do you take it?"
6. **Allergies stage (1 min):**
   - Q7: "Do you have any drug allergies?" (yes/no)
   - Q8 (conditional if yes): "Which drugs are you allergic to, and what reaction did you have?" (distinguish allergies vs. side effects)
7. **Insurance stage (1 min):**
   - Q9: "Do you have insurance?" (yes/no)
   - Q10 (conditional if yes): "What's your insurance name?" → "What's your member ID?"
8. **Summary & confirmation (1 min):**
   - Chatbot displays: "Here's what I captured: [chief complaint], [medical history], [medications], [allergies], [insurance]"
   - Patient asked: "Does this look correct?" (Yes / Need to edit)
9. **Progress indicator:** Visual progress bar shows "Step 2 of 6" or similar; helps patient understand remaining time
10. **Skip/switch option:** If patient gets stuck or frustrated, offer: "Would you like to skip this question or switch to the form?" (graceful fallback)

## Technical Notes

**Requirement Mapping:** FR-005 (Flexible Patient Intake - AI-Assisted & Manual Fallback)  
**Related Requirements:** UI-001 (conversational UX), UX-001 (empathy in design)  

**Conversation Flow Structure (Pseudo-BPMN):**
```
START
  → GREETING: "Hi [Name], intake in 5-7 min. Ready?"
  → CHIEF_COMPLAINT: "What brings you in today?"
  → IF response contains symptom THEN
       → FOLLOW_UP: "How long? How severe?"
  → END_CHIEF_COMPLAINT

  → HISTORY: "Any chronic conditions?"
  → FOR EACH condition mentioned:
       → FOLLOW_UP: "Since when? What meds for it?"
  → END_HISTORY

  → MEDICATIONS: "Current medications?"
  → FOR EACH medication mentioned:
       → FOLLOW_UP: "Dosage? How often?"
  → END_MEDICATIONS

  → ALLERGIES: "Drug allergies?"
  → IF yes THEN
       → FOLLOW_UP: "Which drugs? What reaction?"
  → END_ALLERGIES

  → INSURANCE: "Have insurance?"
  → IF yes THEN
       → FOLLOW_UP: "Insurance name?" → "Member ID?"
  → END_INSURANCE

  → SUMMARY: Display all captured data
  → CONFIRMATION: "Does this look correct?"
  → IF yes THEN STORE_DATA, END
  → IF no THEN ALLOW_EDIT, LOOP_TO_SUMMARY
```

**Question Phrasing Guidelines (A/B Test):**
- Chief Complaint: "What brings you in today?" vs. "What's bothering you?" (Test which feels more natural)
- Medical History: "Do you have any chronic conditions?" vs. "Are you being treated for any long-term health conditions?" (Test specificity)
- Medications: "What medications are you currently taking?" vs. "What pills or inhalers do you use?" (Test clarity)
- Allergies: "Do you have any drug allergies?" vs. "Are you allergic to any medications?" (Test medical jargon)

## Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Flow is too long → patients drop out before completion | Medium | Test with 10 patients; measure average time; trim unnecessary questions; A/B test branching logic |
| Questions are confusing → data quality suffers | Medium | Conduct clarity testing with 5 patients; iterate phrasing; A/B test wording in production (with fallback) |
| Branching logic is complex → chatbot gets lost in conversation context | Medium | Simplify branching; use state machine pattern; document all branches clearly; test edge cases |
| Patient gets frustrated with too many follow-ups → switches to manual | Low | Monitor drop-off rates; measure frustration signals (e.g., profanity detection); offer skip option early |
